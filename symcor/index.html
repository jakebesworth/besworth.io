Jake Besworth - Symcor

This webpage is a reflection on my 2016 Summer work term at Symcor as a Full Stack Web Developer.

Included in my third work term report is an overview of life at Symcor, a description of my job, my goals, and what I got out of it.

Symcor - Workplace

Symcor does a lot of things, but my job consisted of working in the Enterprise Architecture team, on an application used to survey the employees at Symcor. I worked on a team directly under Jack Vanderhorst, also under Ivan Welsh, and worked equally with another co-op student from Guelph, Anand Patel.

Symcor is a really cool place located in Mississauga near Square One. They have a floor with pool, foosball, T.V.s, an icecream machine, a popcorn machine and more. The company at least where I worked is very technology oriented, and the developers and managers are always tech talking. Square One also offers tons of great food locations and places to go, which is but a 5 minute walk from the front door of the building.

Symcor has many meetings, events, and gatherings, all being extremely interesting such as hearing talks from the CEO, or CTO, to having a cowboy themed barbecue with way too much food for me to handle, which was hosted for Symcor. Overall life at Symcor is fun and interesting, experienced, and stable, and I thoroughly enjoyed my summer working there.

Job Description

I was hired for the position of Web Developer with a Full Java Stack with a bit of system administration sprinkled in there. You can see the full list on my resume.

I spent a lot of time implementing or changing features for our web application such as automation, reporting on the back-end or design, performance, and forms on the front-end. I gained a lot of experience working with production ready Java which I had not had experience working with before.

With the sprints that we completed, I can comfortably say that my co-worker and myself made a positive impact at Symcor while we worked there.

Work Term Goals

My first work term goal was my ability to assess, manage, and solve problems at a high level. Due to the myriad of new technologies, this goal was one I saw progress throughout my work term.

My next work term goal was to work on my teamwork abilities due to the fact I was working with another developer directly. I spent the work term improving my communication, and interpersonal skills.

I wanted to work on my ability to understand and use many new and unfamiliar technologies and standards. I was put straight into the fire of Java developmentand it added great experience to my overall ability to quickly familiarize myself with new technologies.

The final goal I gave myself was one most developers seem to understand, which is time management in terms of estimating goals and executing within a time frame. I found myself very able to predict how long tasks would take and what to give as solid estimates throughout my work term, which was my original goal.

What did I get out of my work term?

I gained a lot of new development experience and was able to explore and experience Square One, Mississauga over my work term this Summer.

I wanted to thank Jack Vanderhorst and Ivan Welsh for hiring me and supervising me over the last 4 months. I'd also like to thank Anand Patel for working with me on the application.
