What is Synapp2?

My co-op work term at Dainty Foods consisted of working in the IT department as a software developer under the supervision of Mr. Bob Blanchard, Director of IT.
The work I was tasked with included two projects, the first being "Integrate Synapp2 free and open-source web application generator with Alfresco Community open-source ECM", and the second "Allow Synapp2 compatability with Informix Dynamic Server RDBMS".
My first task was adding a feature that allowed synapp2 users to upload documents to any given alfresco repository through the synapp2 web applications. This involved learning a few new technologies, as well as how to communicate with an API from PHP.

As you may have guessed, this website is a shell of the front-end of Synapp2, with a few changes here and there. Synapp2 is a compact, light-weight web-application generator that is roughly 20k lines of HTML, Javascript, CSS, and PHP. The Synapp2 software was originally created well over 5 years ago by Richard Howell, who I was in contact with during my co-op work term.
The concept behind Synapp2 is you have a database (MySQL, Oracle, Informix) and from that database you create a compact web application with the click of a button, which allows users to perform CRUD operations on the database, and create business level reports on the data.
