My Work Term Goals

One of my goals going into my first co-op work term was to expand my breadth of knowledge of concepts such as database management software (MySQL, and Informix in particular) and web development.

A goal related to the first was simply to improve my SQL literacy in not only writing queries, but understanding relations, normalization and being able to write better, more efficient queries.

The most obvious goal that I planned for myself was to improve my general problem solving skills, such as seeing what techniques my supervisor put to use. I found that problem solving projects in school, and in the workplace are entirely different.
At school you have books, the professor and the TAs as resources to solve your problem, in a very basic sense. At my work I was finding that solutions are a dime a dozen, and implementing the easiest, most efficient, and most applicable solution was much harder than simply finding any one solution.

I also planned a less computer-specific related goal, to impove my ability to communicate my design decisions and implementations via documentation and verbally.
In computer science related fields as I found it, you have to express information in a concise, and detailed manner. Spending a few seconds to describe the actual problem is infinitely more valuable than spending a few hours to get accross the same information but spun a different way.

As my final goal to attain during my work term, I wanted to be able to confidently implement my own design decisions when it came to any project I was working on.
Of course, early on, I found that Bob would tell me a general specification, and I would ask questions along my development. Near the end of the term, I was just implementing my own solutions and he would be pleased with the outcome.

Overall, I think that my original goals were attainable, yet challenging, and I definitely improved my knowledge and abilities in all of the areas listed, and gained invaluable experience in doing so.
