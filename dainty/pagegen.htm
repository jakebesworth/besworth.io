What is Dainty Foods?

Dainty Foods is a primarily rice based company that's origins date back to 1882. Dainty Foods in Windsor was a fairly mid-sized branch, having 20 people in the office and probably less then that in the factory at any given day, but don't quote me on that.
When you first travel to Dainty, you can expect to see open land, the parking lot filled with cars and trucks carrying various food products, and maybe run into a few employees showing up for 8am. The main entrance leads to either Quality Assurance, the factory, the kitchen, or the main office which accommodated management, shipping, and IT.

One thing that is guaranteed at Dainty is that everyone is very helpful and kind. Coming to Windsor, my plan was to take transit to work, which with a bit of waiting, might be an hour travel time. However, most of the employees offered to give me rides to the bus wait, or home, I probably got a ride for 75% of my travels, and I did not ask anyone for a ride once. You could attribute this sort of almost family work environment with the fact that most employees were working there for long periods of time, some approaching 2 and 3 decades! A saying which I heard was "people tend to stay working at Dainty" and that could not be any more true.

Once a year, at the end of the summer, all the employees attend a barbeque, where Jeremy and others cook up a bunch of food, and prizes are given away. With my poor luck, I did not win anything, but overall it was an event to remember. The food was great, the people were happy, and it was a chance to see everyone that worked at Dainty.

Whether you're working in the albeit small, but important IT department, or the factory, Dainty Foods is a welcoming and friendly place that will have you wanting to stay for years. If you're in need of help or even a ride to work, you can count on offers from everyone to help you out.
